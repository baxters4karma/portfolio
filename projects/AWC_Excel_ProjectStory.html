Excel Business Intelligence Analysis for Adventure Works Cycles

Analyzing sales and cost data to provide insights and recommendations

Project Summary

This project involves analyzing three years of sales and cost data for Adventure Works Cycles. The goal is to help the company answer specific business questions and make informed decisions. The analysis includes importing, cleaning, and converting data, conducting table analysis, creating pivot tables, and performing what-if analysis. The findings and recommendations will be summarized in a one-page document.

Project Story

Adventure Works Cycles, a multinational bicycle manufacturing company, is looking to expand its market share by targeting high-volume customers, increasing product availability on the web, and reducing production costs. As a data analyst hired by Adventure Works, my task was to analyze three years of sales and cost data using Microsoft Excel. By doing so, I aimed to provide valuable insights and recommendations to support the company's growth strategies.

Problem/Question to be Solved

How can Adventure Works Cycles expand its market share, increase product availability on the web, and lower production costs?

Process of Getting to the Resolution

  1. Introduction and Company Overview: A brief introduction to Adventure Works Cycles, outlining their market position and strategic objectives.
  2. Understanding the Data: Detailed exploration of the dataset, including sales figures, production costs, and customer demographics. Utilization of Excel for initial data assessment and identification of key data segments for in-depth analysis.
  3. Identifying Business Questions: Formulating specific questions based on AWC’s objectives, such as identifying high-volume sales regions, analyzing cost-saving opportunities, and potential areas for web expansion.
  4. Table Analysis: Using Excel’s advanced features to dissect the data. This involved sorting, filtering, and segmenting data to uncover trends and patterns relevant to AWC’s strategic goals.
  5. Pivot Tables: Creation of pivot tables to dynamically summarize and analyze data, offering insights into sales performance, product popularity, and regional market shares.
  6. What-If Analysis: Conducting scenario-based analysis to predict outcomes of different business strategies, such as pricing changes, marketing campaigns, and cost reduction initiatives.
  7. Summary of Findings and Recommendations: Consolidating all insights and forming actionable recommendations. The summary highlights key findings and suggests strategic steps for AWC to enhance market share and efficiency.

Technologies Used

  • Excel for data processing and visualization
  • What-If Analysis
  • Pivot Tables/Charts

Next Steps

Based on our comprehensive analysis of Adventure Works Cycles (AWC) sales and cost data over three years, we have identified several strategic next steps to support AWC's goals of expanding market share, targeting high-volume customers, enhancing web presence, and reducing production costs.

1. Targeted Marketing Strategies for High-Volume Markets

  • Focus on United States and Australia, where potential for growth in specific product categories like the Mountain-500 bike and the Touring-3000 bike has been identified.
  • Implement targeted marketing campaigns, including discounts and special deals, to boost these products' sales.
  • Actively seek customer feedback in these regions to better tailor our offerings.

2. Referral and Incentive Program

  • Launch a program that incentivizes current customers to refer new clients, aiding in market expansion and customer base growth.
  • Strengthen customer relations and acquire new customers in a cost-effective manner.

3. Expansion of Web Presence and Accessibility

  • Enhance AWC’s e-commerce capabilities to align with global online shopping trends.
  • Improve online shopping experience and increase the range of products available online.
  • Focus on mobile optimization to cater to the growing trend of mobile commerce.

4. Establishing a Foothold in the Asian Market

  • Address underrepresentation in the Asian market by either partnering with a local firm or establishing a new office in the region.
  • Aim to tap into the significant growth potential of the Asian market.

These steps are integral to aligning AWC’s business strategy with market opportunities and efficiency improvements. Continuous monitoring and analysis will be conducted to ensure these initiatives meet their objectives and are adjusted as necessary.
